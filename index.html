"De Windhond"

About

Windmill "De Windhond" is a historical and valuable feature for the city of Woerden.

The Windhond is a round stone mill that was built in 1755 in Woerden. Today the mill is well maintained and is an important part of the cityscape.

The mill stands on one of the last pieces of city wall, originally part of a bastion belonging to the city walls of old Woerden. De Windhond has a flight of 25.2 meters. The mill was restored in 1984 and today it is still in use as a flour mill. The flour from the mill is used at various points of sale. In addition, a number of bakers in Woerden sell bread with flour from the mill. The interior of the mill is largely in authentic condition. The miller lives in the house at the bottom of the mill.
